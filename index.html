2-Lane Shooting Range Build Guide

This guide provides the shopping list, suppliers, and step-by-step instructions for building a 2-lane shooting range with a hybrid backstop using railroad ties and wire mesh.

Shopping List with Recommendations

  • Railroad Ties - 24 ties (from Home Depot or Lowe’s, cost $552 - $720)
  • Wire Mesh Panels - 10 panels (from Tractor Supply Co. or Home Depot, cost $350 - $500)
  • Geotextile Fabric Liner - 1 roll (from Lowe’s or Home Depot, cost $75 - $100)
  • Fill Material (Gravel or Sand) - 6 cubic yards (from Mulch & Stone LLC or JK Enterprise, cost $150 - $300)
  • Fastening Materials - Screws, brackets, wire, zip ties (from Home Depot or Lowe’s, cost $35 - $55)
  • Support Stakes (Optional) - 8 stakes (from Home Depot or Tractor Supply Co., cost $40 - $80)

Assembly Instructions

  1. Prepare the Ground: Clear and level the site, marking a 16' x 4' area for the backstop and 8' x 4' areas for the sides.
  2. Lay the Railroad Ties: Lay 6 ties side by side for the backstop, then stack 2 more layers to form a 4' deep base. Do the same for the sides with 3 ties per layer.
  3. Attach Wire Mesh Panels: Secure 4' x 8' mesh panels to the top of the railroad ties using screws and brackets. Repeat for the sides.
  4. Install Geotextile Fabric: Line the inside of the mesh panels with fabric, securing it with zip ties.
  5. Add Fill Material: Fill the structure with gravel or sand, compacting it evenly throughout.
  6. Finalize the Structure: Secure the tops of the mesh panels with wire or zip ties. Add support stakes if necessary.
  7. Test and Inspect: Ensure stability and perform test shots to check the effectiveness of the backstop.
