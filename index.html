Shooting Backstop Construction Guide

Overview

Constructing a 6-foot-high shooting backstop with wings is a manageable project for 1–2 individuals. Below is a comprehensive guide, including material sourcing near the 20646 and 20774 ZIP codes, required tools, estimated time, and step-by-step instructions.

Materials and Costs

  • Railroad Ties (8 feet long): 24 ties ~$480
    • Home Depot or Tractor Supply Co.
  • T-Posts (10 feet): 14 posts ~$140
    • Available at Tractor Supply Co. or local farm supply stores.
  • Fill Dirt: ~15 cubic yards ~$375
    • Suppliers: Laney Recycling or local landscaping companies.
  • Fasteners: ~50 screws/brackets ~$75
    • Available at Home Depot or Lowe's.

Tools Required

  • Post Driver
  • Shovels
  • Level
  • Drill with Bits
  • Measuring Tape
  • Work Gloves
  • Safety Glasses

Estimated Time

  • Preparation: 2–3 hours
  • Construction: 10–12 hours
  • Total: ~12–15 hours

Step-by-Step Construction Guide

1. Site Preparation

  • Clear the area of debris.
  • Mark the layout with stakes and string.

2. Install T-Posts

  • Place T-posts 4 feet apart.
  • Drive posts 3 feet into the ground, leaving 7 feet above ground.
  • Check alignment with a level.

3. Stack Railroad Ties

  • Lay the first layer of ties horizontally between the T-posts.
  • Stack ties 4 high, securing them to the posts with screws or brackets.

4. Construct Wings

  • Position the wings at a 45-degree angle to the main backstop.
  • Repeat the steps for stacking ties and securing posts.

5. Add Fill Dirt

  • Shovel fill dirt behind the backstop and wings.
  • Compact the dirt to reduce erosion and add stability.

6. Final Inspection

  • Ensure all ties and posts are secure.
  • Clean up tools and materials.
